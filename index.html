FAQ

Some Frequently Asked Question?

Answer: Organic foods are generally good for you and for the environment. Even though organic fruits and vegetables are more expensive than conventional produce, the true long term benefits to health and environment are suggested to be sizeable

Answer: Organic vegetables look similar to conventional vegetables. Since there is no use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides, they may often look natural, smaller, irregular and less attractive. A dot, scar or hole is not uncommon. Identify by trusted source and not the product.

Answer: Organic farming does not employ chemical fertilizers & pesticides, does not utilize sewage water and hence has lower pesticide residues and contamination in food. This translates to safer food. Natural and biological control agents are considered to be less toxic and to leave less or no residues, though this is to be proven conclusively.

Answer: The number one way to tell organic food is by its source – procure from a reliable professional farmer organization that is knowledgeable and trustworthy. Organic food is likely to be less uniform in appearance and may have a scar or a dot. A few insects or worms at times are to be expected. Organic food may not store well for long due to non use of storage chemicals, wax & fungicide coatings.

Answer: Prioritize organic fruits and vegetables which typically have a high use of fertilizers and pesticides or are grown using unhygienic inputs in farms.
